Waking up on Saturday mornings this way was pure joy. I would cozy up with my blanket and cereal while watching old cartoons. I always looked forward to watching Tom and Jerry. I caught old episodes on television and I even had a few tapes. I often imagined the settings while listening to the music. It was a simple show, yet it charmed and captivated the imagination of children. 

The characters were mostly mute, save for a few lines, yet the show was able to engage the audience with storytelling. I even imagined the characters were talking while listening to the music. Silence, for the child watching the show, was a form of storytelling. Exaggerated character actions portrayed and communicated the thoughts and ideas. It was a fascinating form of communication I later appreciated.

The show had a hint of mischief and defiance that enthralled me too. Watching the tiny mouse Jerry turn the tables on Tom on so many occasions and so creatively was a joy. Each time, I relished seeing the delivered the expected outcome no one else thought possible. I was certainly a quiet and timid child, and feeling underestimated is never fun, and feeling one step ahead was something I certainly cherished about Jerry's attitude. His many triumphs instilled the feeling that small was certainly not a synonym for powerless.
