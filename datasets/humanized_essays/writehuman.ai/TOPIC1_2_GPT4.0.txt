Even small ‘flaw’ means school social events and parties, and ‘flaw’ means exclusion. Feeling like they’ve missed out and not being ‘perfect’ can trigger severe emotional distress and heightened suicidal ideation, trigger worsening depression, and self-harm.

It's also worrying that young people are losing the ability to communicate and interact socially in real life. Young people who spend a lot of time on social media may have a hard time adjusting socially in person. People expecting social media to provide real communication are disappointed since social media communication is filtered and often lacks emotional depth. Young people find it hard to have conversations and conflicts in a meaningful way, and they often miss critical social skills, including the ability to identify and understand emotions in other people. This social detachment may lead to problematic isolation and emotional stunting. This isolation limits emotional development and resilience. 

Social media also limits attention spans and affects cognitive development. Social media users, especially young people, have become accustomed to fast-paced content that promote immediate gratification. This may conditioning young people to have poor attention spans, which can have negative effects on the school work. Tasks that require deep work, such as reading, writing, and studying are especially difficult to accomplish. Social media platforms are designed to be addictive, and often young people become so immersed in social media that it limits participation in activities that promote resilience and self control.
